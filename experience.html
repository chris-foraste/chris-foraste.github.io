Academic Background

I’m currently pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering at Tufts University, maintaining a 4.00 GPA and earning Dean’s List honors every semester. I was inducted into IEEE-HKN, the electrical engineering honors society, and have a strong foundation in controls, signal processing, instrumentation, and user-centered design.

I was originally a mechanical engineering major during sophomore year, taking coursework in manufacturing methods, statics, dynamics, thermal systems, controls, CAD, and engineering design. Ultimately, I decided to transfer as of March 2025 in order to pursue electrical engineering fully.

Internship

This past summer, I interned with Enstall, a small solar racking company. Due to the smaller size of the company, I was able to take an interdisciplinary role, working across mechanical, electrical, and software domains. My main project for this internship was improving the speed and accuracy of an ESP-32 based data acquisition system. Through replacing the HTTP communication with Websockets and other code optimizations, the system became five times faster with improved accuracy. Utilizing my user-centered design experience, I was also able to improve the testing experience by implementing features such as automatic log file naming, individualized taring, and a prioritization feature that could turn the 5X speed increase into a 20X speed increase for a specific module.
